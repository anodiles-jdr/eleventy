I was born a delivered soul, and I became a free soul. Without the revolution and without the Travelers, I would never have been able to accomplish this transition — nor live long enough to see its fruits. Today, on Providence, I am responsible for conscription. It is a fundamental role: without a strong, disciplined and unified army, our homeland island would be just another territory offered as prey to the surrounding powers.

All our men and women undergo three years of mandatory training. No privileges, no exemptions. We teach four arts of war drawn from the different cultures of Anodiles:
the navy, defense and engineering, assault, and external operations.
My goal is not to make everyone a hero, but to ensure that all can defend their home — and that none die through ignorance or lack of preparation.

We must not forget that we are a people of peasants: sons, daughters, grandsons, great-granddaughters of farmers. Providence does not, like others, have a long tradition of warrior castes, military orders or lineages of fighters. It is workers of the land that we lead into battle against enemies seasoned in war. And yet… they stand tall. They learn. They become formidable.

We have a deep respect for our conscripts. The program is demanding, but the results speak for themselves. I strongly advise anyone who cares about their life — and the integrity of their face — against provoking a drunken republican in an inn. For a long time, we were sheep offered to wolves. Today, we are bears, and the wolves keep their distance.

Our great innovation was to have allowed everyone to take up arms. And to have established continuous conscription. Every year, between harvests, the entire island enters "war mode". Villages mobilize, clans coordinate, and we carry out maneuvers across the entire territory. This is the best way to maintain our gains, to keep our reflexes sharp and to remind the powerful neighbors that we will never be docile again.

As the person responsible for conscription, I also oversee equipment. We have chosen sobriety. No luxurious crossbows or ruinous chainmail. Our soldiers have a reinforced wooden shield, a heavy sword, greaves, a sturdy helmet. Archers have the same equipment, adapted, and a good bow. That's all. Efficient, economical, resilient. No ornaments, no colors: steel speaks for us.

We are often accused of no longer feeding Anodiles, of wasting our strength on defending ourselves rather than plowing. This is to quickly forget the time when famine was common, when prices soared, and when tens of thousands of us were literally sacrificed.
Those of the ring who participate in our protection are fed without paying.
Those who do not protect us are... merchants. Or feudaries to the Empires.

We chose freedom. And freedom requires maintenance — with sweat, order, and sometimes the saber.
